Why Smart Irrigation is Critical 
for India
Agriculture at the Core
Agriculture contributes ~17% 
of India's GDP and employs 
over 50% of the workforce — 
the backbone of our economy.
Water Crisis
India faces acute water 
scarcity. Agriculture consumes 
80% of freshwater, yet 60% is 
lost to inefficient irrigation.
The Need for Intelligence
Traditional methods rely on guesswork. IoT-enabled systems 
provide real-time, data-driven decisions for precision watering.

Problem Statement
Excessive Water Wastage
Flood and manual irrigation waste 
up to 60% of water due to over-
watering and poor scheduling.
No Real-Time Monitoring
Farmers lack access to live soil 
moisture, temperature, and 
humidity data for informed 
decisions.
Labour-Intensive & Inefficient
Manual monitoring is time-
consuming, error-prone, and 
unsustainable for large-scale 
farming.

Project Objectives
Reduce Water Wastage
Automate irrigation based on real-time soil moisture 
thresholds, eliminating unnecessary water usage.
Automate Irrigation
ESP32-driven relay control enables fully autonomous pump 
operation without manual intervention.
Increase Crop Yield
Precision watering at optimal intervals improves crop health, 
growth rate, and overall productivity.
Real-Time IoT Monitoring
Cloud-connected sensors stream live data to a mobile 
dashboard accessible from anywhere.

Working Principle & IoT Dashboard
Sensing
Sensors measure soil 
moisture and temperature.
Edge Decision
ESP32 reads data and 
checks thresholds.
Irrigation
Relay activates pump when 
moisture is low.
Reporting
Data uploaded and mobile 
alert sent.
The system operates in a continuous loop — sensing, deciding, irrigating, and reporting — with zero manual input required.

Advantages & Limitations
✅ Key Advantages
Significant water conservation through threshold-
based automation
Remote monitoring from anywhere via mobile app
Low maintenance — sensors are durable and cost-
effective
Reduced manpower and operational costs
Scalable from small plots to large farms
⚠️ Current Limitations
Internet Dependency: Requires stable Wi-Fi for cloud 
connectivity
Initial Setup Cost: Hardware investment may be a 
barrier for small farmers
Sensor Maintenance: Periodic calibration and 
replacement needed
Solar-powered deployment and GSM fallback can 
address connectivity and power challenges in rural 
areas.
